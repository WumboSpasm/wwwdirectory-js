ABOUT

This is a JavaScript reimplementation of the World Wide Web Directory CD-ROM by Kris Jamsa and Ken Cope, which was sold alongside the book of the same name. Released in June 1995 and boasting over 8,000 webpages, it is one of the most expansive collections of internet material predating the Internet Archive's formation in May 1996.
Webpages viewed through the site recieve compatibility updates on-the-fly, fixing non-standard and unsupported code while redirecting links to archive sites. While there are no images, screenshots of the pages as they were in 1995 can be viewed through the sidebar. Controls and helpful links are also provided to enhance navigation on and off the site.
This project is not endorsed by or affiliated with Jamsa Press or any of the CD-ROM's authors. Its purpose is to enable research and bring exposure to an obscure piece of internet history. If you would like to download the raw dataset, you can find the extracted CD-ROM files here. The source code for the web frontend can be found here.
